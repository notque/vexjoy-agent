# Design Document Quality Rubric

Rate the design document 1-10 on each dimension:

## 1. Problem Clarity (1-10)
- Does the problem statement explain WHAT needs to be solved?
- Is the problem specific (not vague "improve performance")?
- Would a developer understand what to build from this?

## 2. Requirements Completeness (1-10)
- Are requirements enumerable (checkboxes, numbered list)?
- Are they testable/verifiable?
- Are edge cases considered?
- Are non-requirements explicitly stated?

## 3. Approach Quality (1-10)
- Were multiple approaches considered?
- Is the selected approach justified with rationale?
- Are trade-offs explicitly stated?
- Is the approach feasible given the project context?

## 4. Implementation Guidance (1-10)
- Are components identified?
- Are domain agents specified for implementation?
- Are file paths or areas of the codebase identified?
- Would this guide a plan decomposition?

## 5. Completeness (1-10)
- Are open questions listed?
- Are trade-offs documented?
- Is the scope clear (what's in, what's out)?

## Scoring
- 8-10: Production-ready design document
- 6-7: Adequate but missing some detail
- 4-5: Incomplete, needs significant revision
- 1-3: Insufficient for planning
