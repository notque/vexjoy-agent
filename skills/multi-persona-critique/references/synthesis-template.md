## Section Population Rules

When filling in the template:

1. **Consensus Matrix**: Include ALL proposals and ALL personas. No omissions. Calculate scores arithmetically.

2. **Features You Should Build**: Only proposals scoring 12+. If none qualify, say so explicitly — an empty section is honest; a lowered bar is not.

3. **Worth Investigating**: Score 8-11 only. Focus on the specific conditions that would upgrade the rating. Be concrete ("the Builder needs a migration path") not vague ("needs more thought").

4. **Interesting Disagreements**: The most valuable section. Do NOT resolve disagreements. Present both sides with equal respect. Identify the nature of the disagreement (values vs facts vs perspective).

5. **Shelve**: Brief and respectful. One sentence per proposal. No mockery.

6. **Cross-Cutting Insights**: Look for patterns ACROSS proposals, not within them. Themes, gaps, and tensions.

7. **The Deepest Insight**: One paragraph. This is the synthesis of synthesis — what emerges from the full exercise that no single persona or single proposal could have revealed. If nothing emerges, say "No cross-cutting insight beyond the individual critiques" rather than fabricating profundity.

## Tone Guidelines

| Instead of | Write |
|------------|-------|
| "This idea is terrible" | "This proposal scored 2/15. The Logician and Philosopher both identified [specific flaw]." |
| "Obviously the best choice" | "This scored 14/15 with consensus across all 5 lenses, though the Builder noted [caveat]." |
| "The personas couldn't agree" | "This is contested (score 8/15) with a values-level disagreement between [personas]." |
| "Everyone agrees" | "Consensus at STRONG (score 15/15). Note: shared-model limitation applies." |

**Preserve from persona outputs:** Specific reasoning, evaluation criteria references, conditional statements.

**Do not alter:** Individual persona ratings or justifications. The synthesis adds analysis on top; it never edits what the personas said.
