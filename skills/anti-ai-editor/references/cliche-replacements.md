# AI Cliche Replacements

Complete reference of AI-sounding phrases and their natural replacements.

---

## Tier 1b: Copula Avoidance (AI Verb Substitution)

AI substitutes complex verbs for simple "is/has/are". Sourced from Wikipedia's WikiProject AI Cleanup.

| AI Phrase | Replacement | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| serves as a/the | is a/the | Unless describing actual service role |
| stands as a/the | is a/the | Almost never correct |
| functions as a/the | is a/the | Unless describing actual function |
| acts as a/the | is a/the | Unless describing actual acting role |
| boasts a/the | has a/the | "Boasts" implies bragging |
| features a/the | has a/the | Unless marketing copy (still avoid) |
| offers a/the | has a/the, provides | "Offers" OK when describing options |
| is a testament to | [Rewrite] | "She built X through hard work" |
| stands as a testament | [Rewrite] | Make specific claim instead |
| indelible mark | changed, influenced | Be specific about the change |
| enduring legacy | [Be specific] | What actually endures? |
| profound impact | changed, affected | State the actual effect |
| pivotal role in the evolving landscape | major player, key contributor | Drop "landscape" entirely |
| the future looks bright | [Remove or be specific] | State what's actually planned |
| exciting times lie ahead | [Remove or be specific] | What's exciting? Say that. |
| continues to evolve/thrive/grow | [State what changed] | "Added X in v3.2" |
| poised to | [State what's happening] | "Ships v2 next month" |

## Tier 1c: False Agency (Inanimate Actors)

AI gives agency to inanimate objects to avoid naming who actually did the thing.

| AI Phrase | Replacement | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| the decision emerges | the team decided, we decided | Name the person or group |
| the culture shifts | engineers changed how they... | Name the people changing |
| the conversation moves toward | the team steered discussion to | Someone steered it |
| the data tells us | the data shows (we learned that) | Data doesn't talk |
| the market rewards | buyers pay more for, customers chose | Name the economic actors |
| the complaint becomes a fix | the team fixed it after the complaint | Someone fixed it |
| the narrative shifts | reporters started covering... | Someone shifted it |
| the tone shifts | the speaker changed tone when... | Someone changed it |
| the dynamic changes | [name specific change] | What actually changed? |
| a bet lives or dies | the team killed the project / it shipped | Someone made the call |

## Tier 1d: Observer Perspective (Narrator-from-Distance)

AI narrates from orbit instead of putting the reader in the scene.

| AI Phrase | Replacement | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| nobody designed this | you don't sit down and decide to build this | Put reader in the chair |
| this happens because | your [thing] breaks because | Address reader directly |
| people tend to | you'll probably | Second person is closer |
| one might argue | [just make the argument] | If you'd argue it, argue it |
| observers note | [state the observation] | Who observed? Say it or cut |
| critics point out | [state the criticism] | Unnamed critics are weasel words |

## Tier 1e: Performative Emphasis (Emphasis Crutches)

AI tells the reader how to feel instead of letting facts do the work.

| AI Phrase | Replacement | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| full stop | [remove - the period already stops] | Emphasis through repetition |
| let that sink in | [remove or add context] | Add the "so what" instead |
| make no mistake | [remove] | Just state the claim |
| this matters because | [remove and show why] | If you need to say it matters... |
| here's why that matters | [remove] | The facts should show it |
| and that's okay | [remove or replace with "it works"] | False reassurance |
| i promise | [remove] | Manufactured intimacy |

## Tier 1f: Dramatic Fragmentation

AI uses fragments and staccato for artificial drama.

| AI Phrase | Replacement | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| [Noun]. That's it. That's the [X]. | The whole thing is [noun]. | Trust a complete sentence |
| X. And Y. And Z. | X, Y, and Z. | A list is fine |
| This unlocks something. [Word]. | This gives you [word]. | Don't manufacture reveals |

## Tier 1g: Novelty Inflation / Engagement Bait

AI treats established concepts as newly invented or uses "nobody talks about this" clickbait framing.

| AI Phrase | Replacement | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| the failure mode nobody's naming | [state the actual pattern] | Clickbait framing |
| what nobody tells you about | [just write the content] | Engagement bait |
| the insight everyone's missing | [state the insight directly] | False exclusivity |
| introduced a term | used the term X (dates to [origin]) | Unless genuinely new |
| the thing most people miss | [state what they miss] | Engagement bait |

## Tier 2b: Dangling -ing Clauses (Usually Remove)

Trailing participial phrases that add fake analytical depth. Usually best removed entirely.

| Phrase | Action | Better Alternative |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| , highlighting its importance | [Remove] | Make a separate specific claim if needed |
| , underscoring the significance | [Remove] | State why it matters in its own sentence |
| , emphasizing the need for | [Remove] | "This means we need..." |
| , showcasing the diversity | [Remove] | Be specific about what's diverse |
| , symbolizing the | [Remove] | State the connection directly |
| , reflecting the | [Remove or rewrite] | "This mirrors..." as its own sentence |
| , fostering a sense of | [Remove] | "This builds..." |
| , cultivating a | [Remove] | "This creates..." |
| , encompassing a wide range | [Remove] | "...which covers X, Y, and Z" |

## Tier 2f: Emotional Flatline

AI announces emotions instead of conveying them through specific facts. Earn the emotion; don't declare it.

| Phrase | Action | Better Alternative |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| what surprised me most | [state the surprising fact] | Let the reader be surprised |
| I was fascinated to discover | [state the discovery] | Earn the emotion |
| what really stands out | [state the standout fact] | Facts, not feelings |
| the most surprising thing | [state the thing] | Present it, don't label it |

## Tier 4: Passive Voice Constructions

These aren't always wrong but should be evaluated:

| Passive Form | Possible Active Form |
|--------------|---------------------|
| was performed by | [subject] performed |
| has been implemented | [we/they] implemented |
| will be discussed | [we] will discuss |
| can be achieved | [you] can achieve |
| should be noted | note that |
| must be considered | consider |
| was decided that | [we] decided |
| it was found that | [we] found |
| it has been shown | [research/studies] show |

**When passive is OK:**
- Scientific writing (convention)
- When actor is unknown: "The server was restarted at 3am"
- When actor is obvious: "The code was deployed" (by the team)
- When object is more important than actor

## Tier 6: Context-Dependent (Check Before Changing)

These words are sometimes appropriate:

| Word | Flag When | Keep When |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| seamless | Marketing fluff | Describing actual smooth integration |
| streamline | Vague promise | Specific process improvement |
| empower | Corporate nonsense | Actual capability granting |
| ecosystem | Buzzword | Legitimate technical term |
| scalable | Empty claim | Technical architecture discussion |
| innovative | Self-praise | Describing genuinely novel approach |
| best practices | Generic | Referencing specific documented practices |
| solution | Vague | Describing actual software/system |
| leverage | Corporate-speak | Physics/mechanical context |
| optimize | Vague | With specific metric mentioned |

## Tier 6b: Curly Quotes (ChatGPT-Specific)

| Pattern | Fix | Notes |
|---------|-----|-------|
| \u201C \u201D (curly double quotes) | " " (straight double quotes) | Flag in Markdown, plain text, code |
| \u2018 \u2019 (curly single quotes) | ' ' (straight single/apostrophe) | Flag in Markdown, plain text, code |

**When curly quotes are OK:** Typeset documents, formal publications, `.docx` files, PDFs. Do NOT flag in those contexts.

## Quick Reference: Search Patterns

For quick detection, search for these patterns:

```

delve|leverage|utilize|facilitate|holistic|synergy|paradigm

# Copula avoidance (Tier 1b)
serves as a|stands as a|functions as a|acts as a|boasts a|features a|offers a

# Meta-commentary
In this article|As we've discussed|Let me explain|It's worth noting

# Empty openers
In today's|In our modern|In the world of|In this day and age

# Dangling -ing clauses (Tier 2b)
, highlighting|, underscoring|, emphasizing|, showcasing|, symbolizing|, reflecting|, fostering

# Significance puffery (Tier 2c)
testament to|indelible mark|enduring legacy|profound impact|evolving landscape

# Generic positive conclusions (Tier 2d)
future looks bright|exciting times|continues to (evolve|thrive|grow)|poised to

# Fluff phrases
robust and|comprehensive and|seamless and|cutting-edge

# Redundant emphasis
very unique|extremely essential|absolutely critical|totally

# ChatGPT curly quotes (Tier 3b)

# Dash-as-separator (style ban, weight 2)
# Exclude CLI flags (--verbose, --symlink are NOT separators)

```

## Replacement Decision Tree

```
Is it a Tier 1 phrase?
  YES -> Replace or remove immediately
  NO  -> Continue

Is it copula avoidance (Tier 1b)?
  YES -> Replace with simple "is/has/are"
  NO  -> Continue

Is it meta-commentary (Tier 2)?
  YES -> Usually remove, check if needed for structure
  NO  -> Continue

Is it a dangling -ing clause (Tier 2b)?
  YES -> Remove trailing clause, promote to own sentence if needed
  NO  -> Continue

Is it significance puffery (Tier 2c)?
  YES -> Rewrite with specific claim, or remove
  NO  -> Continue

Is it a generic positive conclusion (Tier 2d)?
  YES -> Replace with specific fact, or cut
  NO  -> Continue

Is it fluff (Tier 3)?
  YES -> Replace with shorter form
  NO  -> Continue

Are there curly quotes (Tier 3b)?
  YES -> Replace with straight quotes (in Markdown/plain-text only)
  NO  -> Continue

Is it a dash-as-separator (em-dash or " -- ")?
  YES -> Is it a CLI flag (--word)? If so, SKIP. Otherwise, rewrite with period, colon, or parentheses
  NO  -> Continue

Is it passive voice (Tier 4)?
  YES -> Check context, prefer active in blog posts
  NO  -> Continue

Is it redundant emphasis (Tier 5)?
  YES -> Remove the modifier
  NO  -> Continue

Is it context-dependent (Tier 6)?
  YES -> Check technical appropriateness
  NO  -> Content is probably fine
```
