# Short Sleep, Sloppy Work: Harbor Institute Study Quantifies the Cost

A new Harbor Institute study has put a number on what tired workers already suspect: participants sleeping less than six hours showed 22% higher error rates on attention tasks.

The study, led by Dr. Mark Ellison, tracked 480 shift workers over twelve weeks using wrist actigraphy and nightly task batteries.

Encouragingly, participants who extended their sleep recovered: after two weeks of longer sleep, error rates returned to baseline.

The $2 million study is the institute's largest sleep project to date.
