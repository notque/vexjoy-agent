# Harbor University Newsdesk: Lost Sleep, Doubled Mistakes

*Campus newsdesk summary, February 20, 2026.*

A Harbor Institute study of shift workers found that errors nearly doubled among workers who skimped on sleep. Researchers say short sleep caused attention lapses that grew worse by the week.

Participants in the study slept under five hours on work nights — far below the seven to nine hours doctors recommend.

The findings add to a growing pile of evidence that night-shift schedules carry hidden costs.
