# Crestline Extension, Six Months In

Six months after opening, the Crestline rail extension is settling into the city's routine. Daily ridership stands at 14,000 — roughly 30% growth since opening, by the agency's own accounting. The extension's final cost came in at $310 million.

Service is frequent: according to the agency's April update, trains run every six minutes at peak.

The project is run by director Lena Cho, a 20-year veteran of the agency. Mayor Otis was less generous in his forecast before opening, predicting the line "would never carry more than ten thousand riders a day."

Boosters point to ripple effects — the line has spurred $200 million in new development along the corridor — and to a technical first: Crestline is the region's first fully automated rail line.
